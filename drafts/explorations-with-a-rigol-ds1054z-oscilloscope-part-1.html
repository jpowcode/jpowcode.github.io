Explorations with a Rigol DS1054Z Oscilloscope Part 1

Sun 06 December 2015
By jpowcode

I thought it was about time I got myself an Oscilloscope. I'm not quite sure what I'm going to use it for yet, but I though I'd find plenty of uses once I started playing around with it. Dave Jones of the EEV Blog reviewed the Rigol DS1054Z and gave it the thumbs up, so that was good enough for me. I'm planning a series of blog posts as I find out the things I can do with this scope. First off I did a bit of Googling and came up with the following places to learn.

  • EEV Blog
  • RigolTech
  • Tektronix xyz's of oscilloscopes
  • Sparkfun tutorial
  • Parrallax understanding signals
  • Tinsel Koala
  • w2aew
  • mjlorton

As I read through and watch these resources I am going to put them in the context of the Rigol scope and write about my findings here.

Testing the Channels

First off I'm going to test the scope works by using the inbuilt test signal. The scope outputs a basic square wave function on the metal terminals at the bottom right of the front panel. Follow these steps

  1. Connect the probe to channel 1.
  2. Make sure the switch on the probe is set to 10x
  3. Connect the black ground clip of the probe to the bottom metal connector of the test signal
  4. Insert the test clip onto the end of the probe and attach it to the top metal connector of the test signal
  5. You may now need to fiddle with the settings to get the scope to trigger. Twist the trigger dial until the trigger voltage is about 1V. The waveform should now stabalise.
  6. By twisting the vertical controls next to the channel dials you can change the vertical voltage scale,
  7. By twisting the horizontal dials you can change the horizontal time scale and show more or less periods of the waveform.
